## What is Vibe Coding?
Okay maybe I jumped the gun and you don't know what vibe coding is. It's basically using an LLM (buzzword hype term: AI) in an environment where it can act as an agent and do all the creation of files and folders and code and whatnot. The human component is meant to supervise (whatever that means).

But I have doubts in this supervisor role. In fact, it encourages non-technical people to build and just give the LLM your idea. Reality is never as simple as that. If the AI or LLM is stuck (and it will get stuck because it hasn't even reached the human brain levels of intelligence), then this non-technical person is, *excuse my french*, *loyally fucked*.

Without the ability to understand the code, which **by the way** was already meant to be written to be understood by a human, one is setting themselves for catastrophic failure.

I do understand the appeal, but at it's current state it is nothing more than a toy.

## Maybe it works
It does work if you're doing something relatively simple and doesn't require security. Things that are disconnected to APIs and servers and completely client-side. Thing's that can be made sloppy so to speak.

It's perfect for things that are unmaintanable.

However, when you start adding complexity, even basic security, or basic fundamental structure of logic that essentially results in security, it just fails.

That's why a lot of seasoned developers are also anticipating that this hype will result in a bloom in the cybersecurity industry. I'm not even in the CyberSecurity course and I could easily *do some magic* on a vibe-coded slop app deployed on the internet once I analyze and find all its issues. It's very easy to spot those by the way because the ones who initiated the application don't know what they're doing and so it'll never get fixed until an actual pro does it for them. Or maybe they could keep on asking the LLM "make it better" but it can't. 

It's obvious how an LLM doesn't have a headspace to visualize logic and design in it's inner workings yet which is extremely important by the way.

## How far are we from a really good Vibe Coder?
Technically this is a question of how far are we until an LLM beats human levels of brainpower.
Sure the LLM runs on hardware that could essentially run faster in "computing." But you see it hasn't
reached "human intelligence" yet. It's obvious no matter how fast at it in regurgitating things that it
is limited.

It can't even tell itself that it's wrong. It doesn't even tell itself that it's unsure or uncertain. Or maybe straight up not know something. It'd rather hallucinate. This humility and awareness keeps people in the next level.

While GPT models may boast trillions of parameters they are heavily dependent on the data they're trained on rather than being able to actually reason and form something authentic. It'll fail easily on unconventional things, or things that have simply never been done before because it has no basis.

What's even worse is energy consumption. The only way to get an LLM to become smarter is to make it larger. Making it larger means requiring more powerful hardware. So much hardware that releases so much heat over time even for trivial use. It's inefficient no matter how we spin it up just to get to measly level not even like a human brain.

Maybe one day things will change, but I feel like we'll run the Earth to dust before we do. The ridiculous amount of heat already having a massive effect and global warming being so apparent. It's a related topic but not the main topic so I'll stop myself for now.

So all in all, LLMs aren't there yet to vibe code a secure application that scales and is maintainable which is pretty much the requirements for any serious industry-leading application.
